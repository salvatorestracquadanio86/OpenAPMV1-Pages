After 10 years working in the field of Asset Performance Management — both from a technical and functional perspective — I've had the opportunity to work with some of the most advanced platforms available on the market and to participate in projects where reliability, maintenance, and asset management were critical business processes.

Throughout this journey, I developed a conviction: **large organizations have access to extraordinary tools.** Highly sophisticated solutions exist that enable companies to build structured processes, collect and analyze data, support decision-making, and continuously improve asset performance.

## The question that started it all

**But what happens when we move into the world of small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs)?**

Very often, I encounter organizations facing the same challenges:

- Maintenance activities that are difficult to track
- Critical knowledge concentrated in the minds of a few individuals
- Documentation scattered across folders, emails, and spreadsheets
- Processes that exist in practice but not within a structured system

Yet the underlying need remains exactly the same: understanding assets, preserving their value, and managing them in a more structured and informed way.

## The idea behind OpenAPM

These reflections led to the idea of **OpenAPM** — a personal project I am pursuing out of passion and long-standing interest in a field in which I have been working for many years.

The term "Open" does not refer to Open Source Software. To me, it means something different. It means envisioning a platform that is **open to a wide variety of organizations**.

Open to companies with complex operational structures that need a flexible foundation on which to build tailored processes.

Open to organizations that would like to introduce Asset Performance Management practices but lack the financial or organizational resources required for large-scale enterprise projects.

> The goal is not to replace or compete with the major players in the industry. Rather, the goal is to explore whether there is room for a solution that makes these concepts more accessible, more gradual to adopt, and closer to everyday operational needs.

## Simplicity as a fundamental value

Another aspect I consider fundamental is **simplicity**.

Many organizations do not have in-house specialists in Asset Performance Management or advanced maintenance methodologies. For this reason, I believe that future systems should be able to guide users through processes, models, and decisions — helping them understand not only *what* to do, but also *why* they are doing it.

Templates, guided workflows, and AI-powered assistance could help bridge the gap between industry best practices and organizations that currently lack access to specialized expertise.

## What OpenAPM is not

I want to be transparent about what this project is and is not.

OpenAPM is a **personal and independent initiative**, with no short-term commercial objectives. The current vision is to make the majority of its functionality available free of charge, allowing professionals and organizations to experiment, evaluate, and contribute ideas and feedback.

Should anyone in the future require significant customizations, specific developments, or consulting activities beyond the project's natural roadmap, these may be considered separately — but they are not the primary purpose of the initiative today.

## What I need from you

Before defining features, priorities, and a roadmap, I would like to hear from people who deal with these challenges on a daily basis.

- How are assets, maintenance activities, and operational knowledge managed in your organization today?
- Do you use dedicated tools, or do you still rely on spreadsheets, general-purpose ERP systems, or internal procedures?
- What is the **first problem you would like to solve**?
